The Eta Aquariid meteor shower favors the Southern Hemisphere; it ranks as one of the finest showers of the year there, in a year when the moon isn’t obscuring the show. At mid-northern latitudes, these meteors don’t fall so abundantly, although people in the southern states in the U.S., for example, tend to see more meteors than those at more northerly latitudes. In a dark sky – when the moon is down – especially at more southerly latitudes, the Eta Aquariids can produce up to 20 to 40 meteors per hour. From mid-northern latitudes, you might only see about 10 meteors per hour.
