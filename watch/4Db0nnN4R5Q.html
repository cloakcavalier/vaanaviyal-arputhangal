Asteroid the size of a football ground is set to fly past Earth on September 14, NASA has said. The big space rock, called Asteroid 2020 QL2, is over 120 meters (393 feet) in diameter. According to NASA's estimates, the space rock could be anything between 53 meters and 120 meters, thus, making it potentially one of the largest rocks on their NEO Earth Close Approaches list. The space rock named Asteroid 2020 QL2 will fly past the Earth and the Moon, and that has been described as a 'near Earth' approach NASA. Going by the higher end of that estimate, it has been suggested that the space rock could be around the same size as the London Eye. During the flying past Earth, the asteroid will be around 4.2 million miles away from Earth. While that might sound far, it’s actually classes as a ‘close approach by NASA.’ The space rock will also be travelling at staggering speeds of up to 23,668 miles/hour.
